Rajgarh Palace

The palace exudes Rajput and Mughal architectural styles with grand courtyards, halls, and balconies, providing an ideal location for royal-themed pre-wedding shoots.

Location Features:

: Situated in Datia town, Rajgarh Palace is one of the oldest historical palaces, known for its magnificent architecture and royal heritage.

Veer Singh Palace

Also known as Govind Mahal, Bir Singh Palace was built in the 17th century and is a fine example of Mughal-Rajput architecture. With seven stories, it remains one of the best-preserved palaces of that era.

Location Features:

: Located on a hilltop in Datia, overlooking the surrounding landscape.

Unao Balaji Sun Temple

The temple is dedicated to Lord Balaji (Sun God) and holds religious importance in the region. Built in a traditional Indian temple style, it attracts numerous devotees and travelers.

Location Features:

: Located in Unao, around 15 km from Datia town.

Sonagiri

Sonagiri is a sacred pilgrimage site with over 100 Jain temples scattered across the hill. The temples are known for their pristine white architecture, creating a visually stunning landscape.

Location Features:

: Situated around 15 km from Datia, Sonagiri is famous for its Jain temples.

Ratangarh Mata Temple

This temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga and is a popular pilgrimage site. The surroundings are serene, with hills, forests, and rivers adding to the charm of the area.

Location Features:

: Located around 60 km from Datia, near the Sindh River.

Peetambra Peeth (Baglamukhi Temple)

Peetambra Peeth is one of the most important Shaktipeeths in India and is dedicated to Goddess Baglamukhi. The temple complex is a peaceful and spiritually charged environment.

Location Features:

: Located in the heart of Datia town

IMPORTANT INSTRUCTION:

Organizing Wedding & Pre-Wedding Shoots in These Locations:

1.Permissions:

oMany of these sites, especially the temples and historical landmarks like Khajuraho and Kalinjar Fort, are protected by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI). You’ll need to obtain special permissions for photoshoots or wedding ceremonies.

o Contact the local tourism office in Chhatarpur or Khajuraho for specific details regarding permits, fees, and regulations.

2.Logistics:

o Travel: Khajuraho has its own airport, with regular flights connecting to major Indian cities. Alternatively, Chhatarpur is well connected by road and rail.

o Accommodation: Khajuraho has a variety of luxury hotels, heritage resorts, and budget stays that can cater to wedding parties and guests.

o Vendors: For wedding or pre-wedding setups, there are numerous local wedding planners, photographers, and caterers available, especially around Khajuraho, as it is a popular tourist spot
